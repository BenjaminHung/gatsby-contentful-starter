The Backdrop of Our Vision

May 11th, 2017

We have been delivering a seven years visionary plan of “Deeper Life & World Mission” for MCAC since two years ago. However, how many of us so far have understood the idea of it and have pursued the vision firmly in our spiritual journeys? It is extremely important for Christians to catch the vision tightly when we are living in the context of intangible and corrupted world. According to the Scripture of Proverbs 29:18, “Where there is no revelation, people cast off restraint.” which tells us the importance of God’s visionary words. People will lose their direction easily without any guidance of God’s words embraced in their hearts. When we have no vision in our lives, we will live in a messy way of under no control as there is no revelation and no God’s will to direct us. We can imagine how messy and confusing it would be in our journey. We just running around every day according to our usual practice.

We thank God for this opportunity we can revisit the 7-year plan of MCAC in the beginning of every year, we are affirmed that “Deeper life” and “World mission” are two main areas of our spiritual growth in 7-year time. They are the big picture, the vision and spiritual compass to point us to the will of God whenwe are standing at a crossroad in everywhere in our lives. “Deeper life and World mission” is the very essence of the Great Commission received from our Lord Jesus.

“All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age.” (Mt 28:18-20)

“Deeper life” is the very part of disciples making and “World mission” is the command of proclaiming Jesus to all nations that they are interrelated to each other. What it means to us is that we cannot make disciples without mission while in the same way we cannot do mission without any sense of disciples making. Because of the fact that they fuel each other to accomplish its good work for the glory of God and edify the body of Christ, we could not cut the Great Commission into half by neglecting any part of it as they are inseparable in nature. When church exists herself in kind of satisfaction of being in a maintain mode, she will be poor in spirit and never experience deeper life in Christ. “Deeper life” and “Mission” are two close friends walking alongside with us in our spiritual journey. They are working in our lives shoulder to shoulder, and they should be pursued together.

Retrospect the year of 2018: Living in the Truth

2018 was the second year of our seven-year plan. It was so important for us to build our spiritual foundation in the words of God. As His word is “...a lamp to my feet and a light to my path.” (Ps 119:105) that is surely the right direction when we are heading to our seven-year plan. The word of God will light up our pathway and root our feet on the rock when we are keep moving forward for His glorious purpose. All is grace from our Lord Jesus who continues to guide us and lead us for building His kingdom. I would like to take this opportunity to extend my warm appreciation to everyone in three congregations for your prayers, contributions and earnest efforts in the past year of your faithful services.

Besides the special revelation of Christ Jesus Himself, the Scripture is another special gift of revelation for the sinful generations. Without His graceful, powerful and righteous words, we couldn’t build godly lives and deep relationship in Him.
